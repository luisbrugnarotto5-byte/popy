## 🔍 What is popy?

popy is a small program that runs in the menu bar on macOS. It remembers what you copy to your clipboard. It stores the last 25 text snippets you copy. When you want to reuse any of those, you open popy from the menu bar and click the item. This helps avoid losing copied text or retyping it.

The application is less than one megabyte. It does not require installation of extra software. popy uses standard macOS tools to work fast and use little system resources.

## 🛠 How to Use popy

After launching, popy sits quietly in the menu bar. It does not open a full window unless you click the icon.

- When you copy text anywhere (press Command+C), popy saves it in its list automatically.
- Click the popy icon in the menu bar to see your copied text history.
- Click any item in the list to copy that text again.
- Right-click an item to paste it directly into the active window without needing to press Command+V.
- The list keeps up to 25 items. Older items are removed automatically.

This lets you recover or reuse text that you copied earlier without going back or rewriting.

## 🔧 Settings and Options

popy offers simple settings to adjust how it works.

- Set whether popy starts automatically when your Mac boots.
- Enable or disable sounds and visual cues when copying or pasting through popy.
- Clear your clipboard history at any time from the menu.

## 🌐 Privacy and Security

popy only keeps text you copy on your machine. It does not send any data over the internet.

Clipboard data is stored locally and cleared when you quit the app or clear history manually.

The app runs with normal user permissions and does not require special access.

## ❓ Troubleshooting

If popy does not appear in the menu bar:

- Make sure the app is in your Applications folder.
- Try restarting your Mac.
- Open popy from the Applications folder again.
- Check the macOS security settings if the app is blocked. Go to System Preferences > Security & Privacy > General, and approve popy if needed.

If popy does not save copied text:

- Check that you are copying text from a standard source (some apps may restrict clipboard access).
- Verify the copy shortcut you use (Command+C).
- Restart popy.

## 📁 Uninstalling popy

To remove popy, follow these steps:

- Quit popy by clicking the menu bar icon and selecting “Quit.”
- Open Finder and go to Applications.
- Find popy and drag it to the Trash.
- Empty the Trash to free disk space.

No other files or settings remain on your system.
